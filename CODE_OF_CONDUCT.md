# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We value the participation of every member and want to ensure that everyone has a positive, respectful experience.

---

## Our Values

This community is built on:

- **Respect**: Treating all people with dignity and consideration
- **Inclusivity**: Welcoming contributions from people of all backgrounds and experiences
- **Collaboration**: Working together to build better educational resources
- **Quality**: Striving for accurate, clear, and helpful learning materials
- **Growth**: Supporting each other in learning and improvement

---

## Expected Behavior

All participants are expected to:

- **Be respectful**: Treat others with courtesy and dignity
- **Be inclusive**: Welcome diverse perspectives and backgrounds
- **Be constructive**: Provide helpful feedback and suggestions
- **Be honest**: Share knowledge and experiences openly
- **Be professional**: Keep discussions focused on the project
- **Be supportive**: Help others learn and grow
- **Report issues**: Raise concerns about violations through appropriate channels

### In Issues & Pull Requests

- Use clear, descriptive titles and descriptions
- Be constructive in code reviews and feedback
- Ask clarifying questions respectfully
- Acknowledge good work and contributions
- Accept criticism gracefully
- Provide context when reporting problems

### In Discussions & Comments

- Keep discussions relevant to the project
- Avoid spam, marketing, or self-promotion
- Don't share personal information without consent
- Respect privacy and confidentiality
- Use inclusive language
- Give people space to learn and grow

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

We will not tolerate:

- Harassment, bullying, or threatening language
- Discrimination based on identity or protected characteristics
- Sexual harassment or unwanted advances
- Hate speech or dehumanizing language
- Deliberate misinformation or spreading false information
- Spam or disruptive behavior
- Violation of others' privacy or intellectual property
- Any illegal activity
- Retaliation against someone for reporting violations

---

## Examples of Unacceptable Behavior

**What we don't allow:**
- "This is stupid code" (use: "I suggest we refactor this section because...")
- Comments about someone's identity, appearance, or background
- Personal attacks or insults
- Unwanted sexual comments or jokes
- Sharing someone's personal information without consent
- Deliberately derailing conversations or trolling
- Exclusionary comments ("You're not a real developer...")
- Political or religious rants unrelated to lessons

**What we encourage instead:**
- "I have a suggestion for improvement..."
- "Can you explain your approach?"
- "I see it differently, but great effort!"
- "Thanks for contributing!"
- Constructive, specific feedback
- Respectful disagreement

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All GitHub issues, discussions, and pull requests
- All communication in project repositories
- All events related to this project
- All interactions where participants identify themselves as part of this project

This applies whether using official project spaces or personal communications about project work.

---

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you experience or witness a Code of Conduct violation:

1. **Report to maintainers**: Email [maintainer-email] or create a confidential issue
2. **Provide context**: Include details about the violation
3. **Protect privacy**: Share what you're comfortable with; we'll treat reports sensitively
4. **Expect follow-up**: Maintainers will respond within 48 hours

You can report violations anonymously if preferred.

### Response Process

1. **Investigation**: Maintainers investigate all reports fairly
2. **Documentation**: Issues are documented and tracked
3. **Communication**: Both parties are informed of findings
4. **Action**: Appropriate action is taken based on severity
5. **Appeal**: Process for appealing decisions is available

### Consequences

Depending on severity, consequences may include:

- **First violation**: Warning and request to cease behavior
- **Repeated violations**: Temporary or permanent removal from project
- **Severe violations**: Immediate permanent removal
- **All cases**: The well-being of community members takes priority

## Specific Guidelines

### For Lesson Content

- Lessons should be accurate and fact-based
- Clearly distinguish between established facts and opinions
- Include appropriate citations and sources
- Acknowledge limitations and edge cases
- Avoid pushing personal agendas
- Respect diverse perspectives while maintaining accuracy

### For Code Examples

- Examples should be complete and functional
- Include clear explanations of what code does
- Use inclusive, clear variable names
- Avoid offensive or discriminatory examples
- Credit sources if adapted from others

### For Feedback

- Be specific about what needs improvement
- Explain why you're suggesting changes
- Offer alternatives or suggestions
- Acknowledge effort even if critiquing work
- Focus on the work, not the person
